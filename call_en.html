1st Workshop on Software Engineering for Avionics System
February 19, 2019, Stuttgart, Germany
Co-located with the SE 2019
Call for Papers
The objective of the workshop is the presentation of methods and technologies of the avionics domain. Topics of interest, but not limited to these, are:
Development technologies:
  • Requirements engineering
  • Modelling languages and tools
  • Transfer of modelling techniques to industrial application
  • Verification via testing and formal methods
  • Security & Safety
Development methods:
  • Certification
  • Agile development
  • Interaction with other domains (e. g. physics, psychology)
Product technologies:
  • Applications of artifical intelligence (including verification)
  • Autonomous Systems
Additional challenges:
  • Reference architectures for hard- and software and interfaces between sub-systems
  • Sensors and sensor fusion
  • Integrated Modular Avionics (IMA)
  • Obsolescence (management)

Assessment and Selection of Submissions
The requested submissions lie in the field of expertise of the Organisation and Program Committee. They are assessed according to the following scientific criteria:
  • Reasonable structure (problem statement and related work, solution, evaluation of the presented approach)
  • Presentation of the unique feature of the submission w. r. t. existing solutions
  • Intersubjectively comprehensible compilation
  • Relevance of the presented approach
  • Innovation of the submission
  • Sustainability of the solution
  • Appropriate citation of relevant scientific publications
  • Form (language, spelling, grammar and formating)
Submission, Presentation and Publication
We welcome two kinds of contributions
Full Paper: The paper does not exceed 8 pages and states completed research.
Short Paper: The paper does not exceed 4 pages and states ongoing research.
Accepted contributions must be presented by one of the authors.
All accepted contributions will be published within the SE- workshop proceedings and be available in COER-WS. The papers must follow IEEE (double column) formatting guidelines and be submitted via EasyChair .
